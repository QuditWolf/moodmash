It must feel like:
- layered collage
- ripped paper textures
- torn magazine cutouts
- film grain
- sticker layers
- paint splashes
- glitch fragments
- weirdcore / indie zine aesthetic when appropriate
- deep artistic references

The artwork must visually merge the user's influences into one cohesive world instead of placing them as separate tiles.

INPUTS:
Username: {username}
Archetype Title: {archetype}

Taste Signals: {tastes}

COMPOSITION RULES:

1. Background Layer
A surreal blended environment influenced by the taste signals.
Examples: liminal hallways, dreamlike landscapes, city nights, retro internet spaces, cosmic surrealism, gallery-like art spaces.
The environment must visually merge multiple influences.

2. Collage Layer
Blend references from the tastes using: torn edges, tape marks, paper fragments, glitch overlays, sticker aesthetics, paint streaks, film burn textures.
Avoid neat rectangles. Everything should feel hand assembled.

3. Avatar Layer (Foreground)
Include a stylized illustrated character/avatar representing the user.
Characteristics: young creative person, expressive, integrated with the collage, wearing headphones / sketchbook / camera / notebook depending on taste signals.
The avatar should feel like the person living inside this aesthetic world.

4. Text Overlay Layer
Include the following typography elements:
- Large username: {username}
- Archetype: {archetype}
- Short summary: {summary}
- Taste tags: Small textual markers referencing the taste signals

Typography style: indie magazine, handwritten notes, cutout letters, typewriter fonts, punk zine text

5. Moodmash Brand Identity (Important)
Every card must contain a shared UI element indicating it was generated by Moodmash.
Use one or more of these subtle elements:
- a small Moodmash sigil / symbol (DNA helix + spark)
- a thin frame line around the card with tiny glyphs
- a Moodmash logo mark at the bottom

Text: "Moodmash"
The branding should be minimal but recognizable across cards.

The visual style must adapt to the tastes automatically.

Examples:
